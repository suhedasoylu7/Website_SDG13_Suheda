SDG 13 - Climate Action

This event focuses on two key climate risks in urban Malaysia: rising heat and frequent flash floods.

By aligning with SDG 13, participants will:

  • Design solutions that reduce climate risk and build resilience.
  • Use data, visualization and geospatial tools to raise awareness and support decision-making.
  • Engage local communities, stakeholders and urban environments in meaningful action.

Resources

  • Malaysian Weather & Warnings API

    From the Malaysian Meteorological Department via Data Gov My. Real-time forecasts and weather warnings which are useful for flood-risk alerts and integrating into apps.

  • Public Infobanjir Water Level & Rainfall Monitoring

    Live rainfall and water level data from Department of Irrigation and Drainage Malaysia, covering flood-prone stations. Ideal for flood-early-warning dashboards.

  • Flood Dataset (Malaysia) 2000-2010

    Historical rainfall data by state/district (for modelling & predictive work) from the Omdena catalogue.

  • Urban Heat Island Dataset - Johor Bahru

    Remote-sensing derived LST, NDVI & NDBI data in Johor Bahru, Malaysia, showing urban heat island patterns. Useful for the ‚ÄúHeat Mitigation‚Äù track.

  • Data Gov My Open API

    A general portal for multiple Malaysian open data sets (environment, disasters, urban-data) where teams can explore additional datasets for their prototypes.
